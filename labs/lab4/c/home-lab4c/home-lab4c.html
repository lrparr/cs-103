Brief History


Hartford, CT is a very historic New England town. It is where the first constitution was written, which has earned us the nickname "The Constitution State".
Puritan settlers led by Thomas Hooker left Massachusetts in 1635, two years after Hooker first left England. He was a minister. But, the Puritans were not the first
settlers of Hartford. Indigenous people well occupied the land (and are responsible for the state's name), and there were Dutch communities as well. Hartford is
one of the thirteen original colonies, was named after a British town, and is the capital of CT.

Until college, I spent my life there. My mom, my grandparents, and my great-grandparents were also raised in Hartford, CT.
